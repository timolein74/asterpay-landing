KYC Policy Explained

Last updated: January 27, 2026

Quick Summary:

AsterPay operates with a risk-based KYC approach. Most crypto-to-crypto payments require no KYC. KYC is required only for EUR off-ramp (fiat bank transfers) and high-volume merchants. All transactions undergo automatic sanctions screening.

When KYC is NOT Required

1. Crypto-to-Crypto Payments (Payers)

If you're paying with cryptocurrency (USDC, USDT, etc.), no KYC is required. You can:

  • Pay from any wallet
  • Make unlimited transactions
  • Use AsterPay services anonymously

Why? Crypto-to-crypto transactions are peer-to-peer and don't involve traditional banking systems.

2. Low-Volume Merchants

Merchants processing less than €10,000 per month in crypto payments:

  • No KYC required for account setup
  • Light onboarding process
  • Start accepting payments immediately

When KYC IS Required

1. EUR Off-Ramp Users

If you want to convert USDC to EUR and receive funds in your bank account:

  • KYC is mandatory for all EUR off-ramp transactions
  • Required by EU regulations (MiCA, AML directives)
  • Bank account verification required
  • Identity verification via our KYC provider

Why? Fiat bank transfers require compliance with EU financial regulations and anti-money laundering (AML) laws.

2. High-Volume Merchants

Merchants processing €10,000+ per month:

  • Light KYC verification required
  • Business registration verification
  • Enhanced due diligence for enterprise customers

KYC Requirements Summary

User Type KYC Requirement Threshold
Payer (crypto-to-crypto) ❌ No KYC Unlimited
Merchant (low volume) ❌ No KYC < €10K/month
Merchant (high volume) ✅ Light KYC €10K+/month
EUR Off-Ramp user ✅ Full KYC All transactions

Compliance & Security

Automatic Sanctions Screening

All transactions, regardless of KYC status, undergo:

  • OFAC screening: US sanctions list checks
  • EU sanctions screening: European Union sanctions checks
  • KYT monitoring: Know Your Transaction - suspicious activity detection
  • AML checks: Anti-money laundering pattern detection

MiCA Compliance

AsterPay is preparing for full MiCA (Markets in Crypto-Assets) compliance:

  • EU-based legal entity
  • GDPR-compliant data handling
  • CASP license application in progress (Q2 2026)
  • Full MiCA compliance planned (Q4 2026)

Learn more about our MiCA compliance roadmap →

Privacy & Data Protection

When KYC is required, we:

  • Use certified KYC providers (Sumsub, Onfido, or similar)
  • Store data in EU data centers (GDPR compliant)
  • Never share your data with third parties (except as required by law)
  • Allow you to request data deletion at any time
